Chocolate Bar Analysis
This project is a product quality analysis, performed by using Python and Tableau.
Image Racool_studio on Freepik
Objective
Chocolate is one of the most popular candies in the world. Each year, residents of the United States collectively eat more than 2.8 billion pounds. However, not all chocolate bars are created equal. In this case study, the objective is to explore what informs the rating of chocolate bars.
Context
Although this data set does not include all kinds of chocolate bars, but only a selected subset, and the ratings reflect a subjective opinion, we will look for one of the reasons that may affect chocolate bar ratings.
Procedures Applied
  • Sourcing open data
  • Exploring relationships
  • Geographical visualizations
  • Regression analysis
  • Cluster analysis
  • Time-series analysis
  • Data visualization
  • Creating data dashboards

Clustering
After our cluster analysis, we came up with 3 main groups of points, which you can see in different colors on the plot.
Differences Between Clusters
On average, the pink cluster has a rating of 3.40. It's followed by a grey cluster with a degree of 3.20 and a dark purple cluster with a degree of 2.70, respectively. The cocoa percentage of the highest rating cluster is on average 71%. While the medium rating cluster has a cocoa percentage of 68%, the cocoa percentage of the lowest rating cluster is 78%, on average. From all this, it can be derived that people don't like to eat intense dark chocolate, in general.
